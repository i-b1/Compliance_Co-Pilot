# HIPAA Privacy & Breach Notification Rule Summary

**Authority:** U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)  
**Statutes:** Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA); Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act 2009  
**Regulations:** 45 CFR Parts 160, 162, and 164  
**Applicable to:** Covered Entities (health plans, health care clearinghouses, health care providers) and Business Associates

---

## Part 1 — Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart E)

### §164.501 — Definitions
- **Protected Health Information (PHI):** individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form that relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual, or payment for health care.
- **Covered Entity:** a health plan, health care clearinghouse, or health care provider who transmits health information in electronic form.
- **Business Associate:** a person or entity that performs functions involving PHI on behalf of a covered entity.

### §164.502 — Uses and Disclosures of Protected Health Information: General Rules
A covered entity may not use or disclose PHI except:
- (a)(1) As permitted or required by the Privacy Rule
- (a)(2) As authorized by the individual

### §164.506 — Uses and Disclosures to Carry Out Treatment, Payment, or Health Care Operations
A covered entity may use or disclose PHI for its own treatment, payment, or health care operations without the individual's written authorisation.

### §164.524 — Access of Individuals to Protected Health Information
- (a) Right of access: Individuals have the right to access and obtain a copy of their PHI in a designated record set.
- (b)(2) Deadline: The covered entity must act on a request for access **no later than 30 days** after receipt.

### §164.526 — Amendment of Protected Health Information
Individuals have the right to have a covered entity amend their PHI. The covered entity must act on the request within **60 days**.

### §164.528 — Accounting of Disclosures of Protected Health Information
Individuals have the right to receive an accounting of disclosures of their PHI for up to 6 years prior to the date of the request.

## Part 2 — Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C)

### §164.306 — Security Standards: General Rules
Covered entities and business associates must:
- Ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all electronic PHI (ePHI)
- Protect against reasonably anticipated threats or hazards to the security of ePHI
- Protect against reasonably anticipated uses or disclosures not permitted by the Privacy Rule
- Ensure compliance by the workforce

### §164.308 — Administrative Safeguards
- (a)(1) Security Officer: Designate a security official responsible for developing and implementing security policies and procedures.
- (a)(5) Security awareness and training program for all workforce members.
- (a)(6) Security incident procedures: Implement policies and procedures to address security incidents, including **identifying and responding to suspected or known security incidents**.

### §164.310 — Physical Safeguards
- Facility access controls
- Workstation use and security
- Device and media controls including disposal procedures

### §164.312 — Technical Safeguards
- Access controls including unique user identification
- Audit controls: hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms to record and examine activity
- Integrity controls to protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction
- Transmission security including encryption

## Part 3 — Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D)

### §164.400 — Applicability
This subpart applies to covered entities and their business associates.

### §164.402 — Definitions
- **Breach:** the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.
- Excludes: unintentional acquisition by authorised workforce member; inadvertent disclosure between persons authorised to access PHI; good-faith belief that the unauthorised person could not retain the information.

### §164.404 — Notification to Individuals
- (b) **Timeliness:** A covered entity shall provide notification to each individual whose unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed as a result of a breach **without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 calendar days after discovery of the breach**.

### §164.406 — Notification to the Media
For breaches involving more than **500 residents of a State or jurisdiction**, the covered entity shall notify **prominent media outlets** serving the State or jurisdiction without unreasonable delay and in no case later than **60 calendar days** after discovery.

### §164.408 — Notification to the Secretary (HHS)
- (b) **Breaches involving 500 or more individuals:** Notification shall be provided **contemporaneously with individual notification and no later than 60 calendar days** after discovery of the breach.
- (c) **Breaches involving fewer than 500 individuals:** Notification shall be submitted to the Secretary no later than **60 days after the end of the calendar year** in which the breaches were discovered.

### §164.410 — Notification by a Business Associate
A business associate shall notify the covered entity of a breach of unsecured PHI **without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 calendar days** after discovery.

## Part 4 — Enforcement Rule (45 CFR Part 160, Subpart C and E)

### Civil Money Penalties (§160.404)
Violations are categorised by culpability:

| Violation Category | Minimum Penalty | Maximum Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Did not know | $100–$50,000 per violation | $1.5M per identical violation/year |
| Reasonable cause | $1,000–$50,000 per violation | $1.5M per identical violation/year |
| Willful neglect — corrected | $10,000–$50,000 per violation | $1.5M per identical violation/year |
| Willful neglect — not corrected | $50,000 per violation | $1.5M per identical violation/year |

---

## Key Compliance Timelines

| Obligation | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Individual breach notification (§164.404) | **Within 60 calendar days of discovery** |
| Media notification for 500+ affected (§164.406) | Within 60 calendar days of discovery |
| HHS notification for 500+ affected (§164.408) | Within 60 calendar days of discovery |
| HHS notification for <500 affected (§164.408) | Within 60 days after end of calendar year |
| Business Associate notification to Covered Entity (§164.410) | Within 60 calendar days of discovery |
| Individual access request response (§164.524) | Within 30 days of receipt |
| Amendment request response (§164.526) | Within 60 days of receipt |
